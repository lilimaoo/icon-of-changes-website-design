About The Program

The Forest Restoration and Rehabilitation program is a flagship initiative of Icons of Change aimed at combating deforestation and land degradation in critical ecosystems across the Philippines. This comprehensive program combines reforestation, agroforestry, and community engagement to restore degraded forests while creating sustainable livelihoods for local communities.

Launched in 2020 in response to the increasing rates of forest loss in Central Luzon, this program takes a holistic approach to environmental restoration by addressing both ecological needs and human factors that contribute to deforestation.

Since its inception, the Forest Restoration and Rehabilitation program has made significant strides in restoring critical forest ecosystems while creating positive socio-economic impact for local communities.

Key Components

  • Targeted Reforestation: Strategic planting of native tree species in degraded areas, focusing on biodiversity hotspots and watershed protection zones.
  • Agroforestry Systems: Development of integrated land-use systems that combine trees with agricultural crops, providing both ecological benefits and income opportunities.
  • Nursery Establishment: Creation of community-managed nurseries for native tree species, serving as both training centers and seedling production facilities.
  • Community Forest Management: Training and capacity building for local communities to sustainably manage forest resources through participatory approaches.
  • Alternative Livelihood Development: Introduction of sustainable livelihood options that reduce pressure on forest resources while improving economic conditions.
  • Environmental Education: Comprehensive awareness programs for schools and communities on forest conservation and sustainable practices.
